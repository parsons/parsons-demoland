CSS Flexbox display: grow, shrink, and basis

flex-grow and flex-shrink apply to the item level, where the elements will respond to the container space responsively. As a starting point to understand their basic values, you can see 1 as "yes (true)", and 0 as "no (false)". For instance, flex-shrink: 1; means this item will shrink when the container becomes smaller, and flex-grow: 0; means this item will not grow even when there is extra space in the container.

flex-basis also applies to the item level, where it sets the initial size of the item on the main-axis — when flex-direction is row, flex-basis controls width, when flex-direction is column, flex-basis controls height.
